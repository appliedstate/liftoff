# Batch 01 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 10

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 10

### Change

Built the intent-packet exploration ownership layer: canonical packet queue storage in monitoring, mutation endpoint, backend report, and operator surface for packet-review cadence.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Founding Partner Seat`

Why this seat:

This is the most direct operating answer to the repeated concern that packet cracking is strategically important but still under-owned.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Before this change, the system could see packet-level learning:

- which packets were showing signal
- which were noisy
- which were economically positive

But it still could not answer the most important operating question:

who is actually responsible for moving the promising packets forward?

### Why Now

The upstream workstream scoreboard already proved that packet learning exists.

The next bottleneck was no longer awareness, but cadence:

- who reviews it
- when they review it
- whether a promising packet is being explored, tested, or exploited

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`intent-packet exploration was visible, but still not operated as a disciplined growth function`

More specifically:

the founder-priority packet-cracking loop still lacked explicit owner, queue state, and review timing.

### Operator Read

This change should let the operator answer:

1. Which packet clusters are unowned?
2. Which packet reviews are overdue?
3. Which packets have positive signal but weak cadence?
4. Whether packet exploration is being run as a governed function instead of ambient research.
