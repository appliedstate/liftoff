# Batch 02 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 6

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 6

### Change

Built the buyer throughput and follow-through layer into canonical buyer scorecards so each buyer now carries recent action movement, recent opportunity review/approval/launch conversion, blueprint creation velocity, closure rate, launch follow-through rate, and a compact throughput band.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Jeff Bezos`

Why this seat:

This change is about whether work actually moves through the system instead of just accumulating in queues, which is a mechanism and follow-through question more than a reporting question.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Before this change, the scorecard could show:

- queue pressure
- opportunity inventory
- launch activity
- explore vs exploit behavior

But it still could not show whether a buyer was actually moving work forward:

- closing actions
- reviewing owned opportunities
- converting approved work into launches
- maintaining throughput instead of just carrying backlog

### Why Now

Once constrained surfaces were visible, the next missing truth was execution motion.

That matters now because a buyer can look busy, look constrained, or look well supplied without actually demonstrating follow-through.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`buyer scorecards still could not distinguish backlog from real throughput`

More specifically:

the operator could see open actions and owned opportunities, but not whether those items were advancing, closing, or converting into launch output.

### Operator Read

This change should let the operator answer:

1. Which buyers are actually closing work recently?
2. Which buyers are reviewing and converting owned opportunities?
3. Where is work moving but not visibly finishing?
4. Which buyers have backlog without follow-through?
