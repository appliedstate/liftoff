# Batch 02 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 5

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 5

### Change

Built the constrained-surface exposure layer into canonical buyer scorecards so each buyer now carries a grounded surface-risk block showing linked account surfaces when known, inferred platform exposure when unresolved, and active constraint pressure from the platform-capacity registry.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Mark Zuckerberg`

Why this seat:

This change is about separating buyer performance from platform-surface reality, especially when Meta and other buying surfaces are constrained, degraded, or restricted.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Before this change, a weak buyer scorecard could still be causally ambiguous:

- was the buyer weak
- or were they operating on a damaged account surface
- or were they exposed to Meta / NewsBreak / MSN without the system making that visible

Without this layer, the scorecard still risked blaming the buyer for platform damage.

### Why Now

Once the scorecard had:

- buyer economics
- attribution confidence
- opportunity mix
- explore vs exploit behavior

the next missing truth was surface reality.

That matters now because allocator and operator decisions become directionally wrong if they ignore restricted or degraded buying surfaces.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`buyer scorecards still could not separate execution quality from constrained account surfaces`

More specifically:

the operator could see who was performing, but not whether a constrained Meta business manager, account hygiene problem, or unresolved platform exposure was distorting that read.

### Operator Read

This change should let the operator answer:

1. Which buyers are directly attached to constrained account surfaces?
2. Which buyers show platform exposure even when the exact account is not yet resolved?
3. Where is surface risk likely contaminating the scorecard read?
4. Which weak scorecards may be surface-impaired rather than purely buyer-impaired?
