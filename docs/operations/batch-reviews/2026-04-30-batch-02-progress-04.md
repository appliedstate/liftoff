# Batch 02 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 4

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 4

### Change

Built the net-new vs recycled launch scorecard layer as an `Explore vs Exploit` buyer layer: the scorecard now carries recent launch activity, launch-category/source activity, and an estimated split between launches backed by owned opportunity exploration versus launches happening on already-known exploit-side behavior.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Founding Partner Seat`

Why this seat:

This change directly encodes the founder concern that buyers may be optimizing easy exploit paths instead of generating and converting real new supply.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Before this change, the scorecard could show:

- economics
- attribution quality
- execution pressure
- owned opportunity inventory

But it still could not show:

- whether a buyer was actually exploring
- whether launches were mostly exploit-side
- whether activity was broadening into new categories
- whether launch behavior was active or inert

### Why Now

Once opportunity mix was attached to the scorecard, the next missing truth was behavior:

was the buyer converting net-new exploration, or mostly running the exploit lane?

That is the core question behind the founder’s upstream concern.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`buyer scorecards still could not distinguish exploration behavior from exploit behavior`

More specifically:

the operator could see performance and owned inventory, but not whether recent launch activity reflected new supply creation or mainly exploitation of existing patterns.

### Operator Read

This change should let the operator answer:

1. How many recent launches did each buyer create?
2. How active is each buyer in terms of launches, categories, and sources?
3. Is the buyer currently more explore-heavy or exploit-heavy?
4. Is launch activity broadening the system or mostly running the easy lane?
