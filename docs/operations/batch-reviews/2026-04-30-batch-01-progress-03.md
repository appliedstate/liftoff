# Batch 01 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 4

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 4

### Change

Built the first operator-facing opportunity and intent-packet workstream scoreboards so upstream exploration is visible as a control surface rather than a belief.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Founding Partner Seat`

Why this seat:

This change exists because the founder concern was already explicit:

- not enough opportunity sniffing
- not enough focused intent-packet cracking
- too much bias toward what is easy to launch

This scoreboarding layer is the first system response to that concern.

### Why It Needs To Exist

The repo already had the primitives:

- opportunity queue
- campaign blueprints
- intent-packet learning observations

But those primitives were still too dark at the operating-system level.

Without a scoreboard:

- exploration work looks optional
- upstream bottlenecks stay anecdotal
- opportunity supply cannot be reviewed like a real workstream
- intent-packet learning cannot be distinguished from sporadic experimentation

This change makes the workstream inspectable.

### Why Now

The board’s approved sequence put this immediately after canonical buyer scorecards.

That order is correct because:

- buyer truth tells us who is performing
- exploration truth tells us whether the system is creating enough future supply

You need both if the operating system is going to improve durable net profit rather than just report on current buyers.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`weak visibility into upstream opportunity generation and intent-packet exploration`

More specifically:

the business had partial primitives, but not a board-level view of whether those workstreams were alive, stalled, noisy, or ownerless.

### Operator Read

This change does not pretend the ownership problem is solved.

In fact, part of its job is to make that gap obvious.

If it works, the operator should now be able to answer:

1. Is there real pending opportunity supply?
2. Is it converting into blueprint and launch motion?
3. Is intent-packet learning active or dark?
4. Are we dealing with a supply problem, a noise problem, or an ownership problem?
