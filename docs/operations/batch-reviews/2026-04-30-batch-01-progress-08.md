# Batch 01 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 9

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 9

### Change

Built the opportunity ownership queue: canonical queue storage, mutation endpoint, backend report, and operator surface for opportunity conversion ownership.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Jeff Bezos`

Why this seat:

This is not a visibility problem anymore.

It is a mechanism problem:

important opportunities now need owner, next step, aging, and conversion accountability.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Before this change, the system could see opportunities and even rank them.

But it could not govern the crucial middle:

- who owns this opportunity
- what is the next required step
- whether it is aging without blueprint conversion

That meant discovery could still happen without disciplined downstream follow-through.

### Why Now

Now that opportunity supply is visible and stale pending items are measurable, the next missing primitive is the ownership queue itself.

Without this layer, the system still knows which opportunities matter but cannot force them through a controlled pipeline.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`opportunity discovery existed, but conversion accountability was still weak`

More specifically:

the business had ranked opportunity inventory, but no first-class operating queue that made ownership, cadence, and blueprint progression explicit.

### Operator Read

This change should let the operator answer:

1. Which opportunities are still unowned?
2. Which pending opportunities are stale?
3. Which ones are blueprinting versus stalled?
4. Where is opportunity-to-blueprint conversion actually breaking?
