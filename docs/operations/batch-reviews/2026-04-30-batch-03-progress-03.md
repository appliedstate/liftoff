# Batch 03 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 03

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 03

### Change

Fed opportunity-supply quality back into canonical buyer scorecards and allocator grounding, so weak exploration quality now degrades buyer health and can block growth posture.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Jim Simons`

Why this seat:

This change makes capital decisions more causally correct by preventing bad upstream supply from masquerading as healthy exploration.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Before this change, the system could score:

- opportunity volume
- pending upside
- buyer exploration activity

But it still risked confusing quantity with quality.

That meant the allocator could see lots of upstream motion and still miss that the supply engine was producing stale or weak inventory.

### Why Now

After building the opportunity supply quality loop, the next highest-leverage move was to attach that signal to the places where decisions are actually made:

- buyer scorecards
- allocator posture

Otherwise the quality loop would remain advisory instead of operational.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`allocation and buyer health could still over-credit upstream supply volume without testing supply quality`

More specifically:

the system could see exploration activity, but it did not yet penalize stale low-conversion supply strongly enough in core capital and operator reads.

### Operator Read

This change should let the operator answer:

1. Is this buyer’s upstream supply actually healthy?
2. Should poor supply quality hold back growth even if volume looks good?
3. Is the allocator now responding to exploration quality rather than just exploration presence?
4. Which buyer lanes are being distorted by low-quality upstream inventory?
