# Batch 01 Progress Review — Meaningful Change 5

This packet is designed for operator review in under two minutes.

## Change 5

### Change

Built the first execution-gap tracker that turns recurring concerns, ownerless action items, unresolved questions, and meeting-level follow-through failures into an explicit operator surface.

### Responsible Board Seat

`Jeff Bezos`

Why this seat:

This is a mechanisms problem.

The point is not to summarize that a concern exists.

The point is to create a mechanism that exposes where the system is still failing to convert concern into owned execution.

### Why It Needs To Exist

Meeting intelligence was already capturing:

- voice signals
- action items
- open questions

But without an execution-gap layer, those signals were still too fragmented.

That meant:

- repeated concerns could remain diffuse
- ownerless work could hide in meeting detail
- unresolved questions could survive without becoming visible operating debt

This change makes execution leakage inspectable.

### Why Now

The board sequence explicitly put this after scorecards and exploration visibility.

That timing is correct because:

- buyer scorecards tell us who is under pressure
- exploration scoreboards tell us whether upstream supply is alive
- execution-gap tracking tells us whether the organization is still dropping known work on the floor

Without this layer, the operating system still cannot answer whether follow-through is actually improving.

### Limiting Factor It Tackles

Primary limiting factor:

`follow-through leakage between recurring concern, ownership, and completion`

More specifically:

the system had pieces of the signal, but not a first-class view of repeated concerns without owners or questions without closure.

### Operator Read

This change is not about more narrative.

It is about making execution debt legible enough that the operator can ask:

1. Which concern themes keep repeating?
2. Which ones still have no owner?
3. Which meetings are generating the most unresolved residue?
4. Are we failing because we do not know the problem, or because we are not assigning it?
