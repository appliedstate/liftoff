# Purpose

This memo records the board-guided lean-coffee answer to:

What should Liftoff work on next after:

- buyer attribution audit
- founder-private conversation lane

while staying faithful to the prime directive and the current batch goal of grounding buyer performance to the scorecard.

# Prime Directive

Maximize durable net profit growth per unit of constrained company capacity, while never allowing projected monthly net profit to fall below the configured floor.

# Current Built State

The system now has:

- canonical buyer scorecards
- buyer attribution audit
- opportunity and intent-packet ownership queues
- meeting intelligence and execution follow-through
- allocator grounding
- founder-private conversation lane

The system can now see more of operating reality.

The next bottleneck is that buyer performance is still not decomposed enough to tell:

- whether a buyer is strong because of skill or easy mix
- whether a buyer is weak because of bad execution or toxic surfaces
- whether capital should move because of buyer quality or merely because of favorable inherited conditions

# Lean Coffee Order

## 1. Buyer Opportunity Mix Layer

### Why It Needs To Exist

A buyer scorecard still does not show enough about what kind of opportunities a buyer is actually touching.

Without this, the operator cannot tell whether:

- a buyer is building net-new supply
- a buyer is recycling proven categories
- a buyer is sitting on easy inherited opportunities

### Why Now

The attribution audit made the ownership problem explicit.

The next truth problem is mix:

performance by itself still hides what kind of portfolio the buyer is actually carrying.

### Limiting Factor

`buyer economics are visible, but buyer opportunity mix is still opaque`

### Responsible Board Seats

- `Warren Buffett`
- `Jim Simons`
- `Founding Partner Seat`

## 2. Net-New Vs Recycled Launch Scorecard Layer

### Why It Needs To Exist

The system should distinguish:

- launches from net-new opportunities
- launches from recycled known winners
- launches from cloned / derivative surfaces

Otherwise throughput can look healthy while true exploration is still weak.

### Why Now

Founder concern has consistently pointed upstream:

weak sniffing, weak packet cracking, too much local optimization.

This is the scorecard layer that makes that concern measurable per buyer.

### Limiting Factor

`launch volume exists, but exploration quality is not yet measurable at the buyer level`

### Responsible Board Seats

- `Founding Partner Seat`
- `Jeff Bezos`
- `Mark Zuckerberg`

## 3. Constrained-Surface Exposure Layer

### Why It Needs To Exist

Some buyer outcomes are distorted by the surfaces they are forced to operate on:

- restricted accounts
- noisy business managers
- weak tracking
- risky redirect / domain surfaces

The scorecard should show how much of a buyer’s result is happening under constrained conditions.

### Why Now

Platform capacity and risk are already modeled.

What is missing is joining those constraints back to buyer performance in a way the allocator can use.

### Limiting Factor

`buyer performance is still not normalized for surface quality or constraint exposure`

### Responsible Board Seats

- `Mark Zuckerberg`
- `Warren Buffett`
- `Jensen Huang`

## 4. Buyer Throughput And Follow-Through Layer

### Why It Needs To Exist

The scorecard should include not just economics, but whether a buyer is actually moving work through the system:

- open follow-through load
- time from opportunity to launch
- stalled queue burden
- ownership discipline

### Why Now

The meeting and queue systems already capture most of the raw material.

The missing step is turning it into buyer-level scorecard truth instead of leaving it in separate reports.

### Limiting Factor

`execution drag is visible in separate systems, but not yet attached tightly enough to buyer evaluation`

### Responsible Board Seats

- `Jeff Bezos`
- `Patrick Collison`
- `Founding Partner Seat`

## 5. Allocation Trigger Policy

### Why It Needs To Exist

Once the scorecard is truer, the operator needs explicit policy for what happens next.

Examples:

- when to protect
- when to hold
- when to cautiously grow
- when to scale

### Why Now

This should come only after mix, exploration quality, and constraint exposure are incorporated.

Otherwise the policy will still be acting on partial truth.

### Limiting Factor

`allocator posture exists, but the trigger policy is still too advisory and under-specified`

### Responsible Board Seats

- `Jim Simons`
- `Warren Buffett`
- `Patrick Collison`

# Board Synthesis

## Founding Partner Seat

Do not let buyer scorecards become a dressed-up P&L only.

They need to expose whether buyers are really creating supply, exploring, and pushing into hard areas or just surfing easy inventory.

## Jeff Bezos

The next win is mechanism, not more prose.

Make the scorecard answer operationally expensive questions directly so capital and intervention can follow without interpretation overhead.

## Jim Simons

The next problem is causal contamination.

Buyer outcomes need better decomposition before the allocator can trust them more deeply.

## Warren Buffett

Performance should be judged with context:

- what portfolio was carried
- how much risk was embedded
- whether the result came from durable edge or inherited advantage

## Mark Zuckerberg

The system already knows constrained surfaces exist.

The scorecard now needs to show which buyers are carrying those surfaces so the operator does not misdiagnose the person when the environment is the problem.

## Patrick Collison

The next build should reuse existing data, not invent a new reporting universe.

Most of what is needed already exists in:

- attribution audit
- opportunity ownership
- platform capacity
- execution queues

The work is to bind them into a canonical buyer-performance object.

# Decision

The board’s lean-coffee recommendation is:

## Next Build Order

1. `Buyer Opportunity Mix Layer`
2. `Net-New Vs Recycled Launch Scorecard Layer`
3. `Constrained-Surface Exposure Layer`
4. `Buyer Throughput And Follow-Through Layer`
5. `Allocation Trigger Policy`

# Final Answer

The next work should stay inside `buyer performance to scorecard grounding`.

Do not pivot away yet.

The highest-leverage move is to make the buyer scorecard tell a truer causal story before adding more allocator sophistication or more private-lane integrations.
