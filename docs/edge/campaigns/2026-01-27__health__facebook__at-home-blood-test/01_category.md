## 01 — Category

- **Vertical**: health
- **CategorySlug**: at-home-blood-test
- **Category definition (1–2 sentences)**:
  - People who want **health clarity** without a doctor visit: a simple at-home kit, clear results, and next-step guidance.
- **Promise (single sentence)**:
  - “Get a clear snapshot of key health markers from home.”
- **Proof mechanism**:
  - Simple, step-by-step process + “lab testing” positioning (avoid over-claiming accuracy; no diagnosis/cure claims).
- **Pathway**:
  - Meta video → pre-sell / explainer page → kit selection → checkout → results education (post-purchase).
- **Market**:
  - US (confirm state restrictions if applicable).
- **Policy/compliance constraints**:
  - No diagnosis, cure, or guaranteed outcomes.
  - Avoid “you have X” or “this detects cancer/diabetes” type claims.
  - Use soft language: “may help you understand”, “for informational purposes”, “not medical advice”.
  - Ensure disclaimers appear in ad + on landing page.
- **Success metric (economic)**:
  - Primary: Session ROAS / vRPS vs CPS (if RSOC economics).
  - If pure ecommerce: ROAS + CPA with refund/chargeback guardrails.

1) **Clarity / peace of mind**: “Know your numbers without the waiting room.”
2) **Convenience**: “From couch to results—no appointment.”
3) **Actionable next steps**: “Results explained with simple recommendations.”

### Optimization point (where the loop should tighten first)

**Optimize first at the ad-level “attention → qualified click” layer**, because it’s the fastest feedback:
- Hook clarity (first 2 seconds)
- CTR and thumbstop proxy (3s views / video completion)
- Landing page match (CTR good but CVR weak = message mismatch)

Then optimize **economic truth** at the campaign level:
- Spend → sessions → revenue (vRPS / Session ROAS) or Purchase value ROAS

### Data-to-asset feedback map (what metrics should change what)

- **Low CTR / weak hold rate** → rewrite hooks/overlays; test new angle (ad-level).
- **Good CTR / weak CVR** → tighten claim alignment on LP; adjust “what happens next” clarity.
- **Good CVR / weak economics** → adjust offer structure (bundle, price anchoring) and qualification.
- **High refunds** → set expectations in ads; add “who it’s for / not for”.
