## What we’re optimizing for (be explicit)

We are optimizing for the RSOC chain:

**Stop scroll (0–1s)** → **Earn click to article (1–8s)** → **Earn widget click (in-article)** → **Earn downstream advertiser click**

The copy job is *not* “sell the product.” It’s:
- create **high-intent curiosity**
- with **low friction**
- while staying inside **AdSense/RSOC + Meta policy**
- and ensuring **ad claims ⊆ article claims**

## The Direct Marketing Persona (how we sound)

**Persona name**: *The Calm Contrarian Operator*

- **Voice**: calm, surgical, specific. No hype, no moralizing, no cringe.
- **Vibe**: “I’m not selling you — I’m showing you the first step most people skip.”
- **Promise style**: informational + procedural (what you’ll SEE / what you’ll LEARN), not outcomes (“this will fix…”).
- **Truth constraint**: every claim must be supported by the **article**; article must be AdSense/RSOC safe.
- **Emotion**: relief (“finally, clarity”) + control (“do this before you guess”).
- **Mechanism**: curiosity + specificity + contrarian reframe (not fear).

## “Guide” isn’t the CTA (micro-commitment is)

You’re right: “read a guide” can feel like work.

Instead, we offer **micro-commitments** that feel effortless:
- “See the 3-step checklist”
- “See the first thing to check”
- “See the 10-second explanation”
- “See what this includes”
- “See the quick breakdown”
- “See the list (then decide)”

**Why this works (no fake stats)**:
- There are no universal “people love guides” statistics that reliably transfer across niches.
- What *is* reliable is human behavior: people click when the next step is **easy** and the payoff is **specific**.
- So we phrase the click as “see one thing” rather than “do homework.”

## Alignment contract (non-negotiable)

Copy must align at every handoff:

- **Ad hook** ↔ **Article headline** ↔ **Article section headers** ↔ **Widget phrases (3–4)** ↔ **SERP intent**

If CTR is high but widget clicks / vRPS are weak, you likely broke alignment or over-promised.

## Widget framing (critical for RSOC policy + promise integrity)

Treat widget buttons as **search phrases / next-step terms**, not “offers”.

Use language like:
- “tap a search phrase”
- “see common search terms”
- “pick the phrase that matches what you’re looking for”

Avoid language that implies an in-article marketplace:
- “choose the option that fits”
- “pick your best option”
- “compare prices here”
- “get quotes now”

## Hook formulas (8–10s ads) — high response without prohibited claims

Short ads are not “mini VSLs.” They’re **pattern interrupts + micro-payoffs**.

Use this structure for short video:

1) **Pattern interrupt** (0–1.5s): a specific tension (no “you” diagnosis)
2) **Contrarian reframe** (1.5–3s): why the obvious move is wrong (“don’t guess / don’t chase hacks”)
3) **Specific micro-payoff** (3–7s): a numbered list, checklist, “first step”
4) **Micro-commitment CTA** (7–10s): “tap to see the list / see the breakdown”

### Hook templates (policy-safe)

Use **specificity** and **contrast**:

- “Everyone’s guessing about **[symptom]**. Here’s the **first thing to check**.”
- “Before trying another **hack**, check this **3-step baseline**.”
- “The easiest first step isn’t a supplement — it’s **a baseline**.”
- “If the internet is noisy, start with a **simple checklist**.”

### Stronger pattern interrupts (visual-first)

Use overlays people recognize instantly:
- “STOP guessing” / “Don’t chase hacks”
- “3 things to check first”
- “Baseline > opinions”
- “Measure → then decide”

## “Compelling without claims” levers (use 2–3 per ad)

- **Specificity**: “3 things”, “first step”, “quick checklist” (avoid “60-second guide” if it feels like work)
- **Contrarian**: “stop guessing”, “measure first”
- **Curiosity gap**: “the step most people skip”
- **Process proof**: show the article headline + the 3 widget phrase buttons on screen
- **Micro-commitment**: “tap to see the list”
- **Social proof without claims**: “common starting point” / “what people usually check first” (no numbers unless true)
- **Anti-hype**: “No promises. Just a clean starting point.”

## Disclosures without killing response (how we handle “not medical advice”)

We do **not** put “Not medical advice” in headlines.

Instead we use a layered approach:
- **Primary**: keep the headline clean and curiosity-driven.
- **Secondary**: add a **small, non-annoying footer line** on the article (and optionally in the video caption).
- **Tertiary**: if the vertical/account requires it, add **tiny on-screen text** (small, bottom, 1–2 seconds), not as the main CTA.

Use softer phrasing when appropriate:
- “For informational purposes”
- “Educational content”

## On-screen text rules (this matters more than VO)

Use 3 layers:
- **Big headline** (matches article headline)
- **One payoff line** (matches article section header)
- **CTA** (“See the checklist →” / “See the breakdown →”)

Always include small text:
- “For informational purposes” (or “Not medical advice” only if required)

## Policy filter (hard gates)

- No “you have…”, “your condition…”, “are you suffering…”
- No diagnosis/certainty (“this detects…”, “this means you have…”)
- No guarantees (“will”, “guaranteed”, “instant”, “cure”)
- No fear/scare certainty
- Ad claims must be supported by the article (claim map)

## Swipe file: 8–10s scripts that tend to pull (fill with your category)

Use these as “shells” (then map claims to the article):

1) **Don’t guess → see 3 checks**
   - “STOP guessing about **[topic]**. Here are the **3 things** most people check first. Tap to see the list.”
2) **Anti-hack**
   - “Before trying another **[hack]**, get a baseline. Tap to see what to check first.”
3) **Checklist reveal**
   - “This is the simplest starting point: **[X] checklist**. Tap to see it.”
4) **Reframe**
   - “The problem isn’t **[symptom]** — it’s guessing. Measure first. Tap to see the breakdown.”

## Scoring rubric (so we know what “good” is)

Score each ad concept 0–2 on each dimension (max 10):
- **Alignment**: hook matches article headline + widget phrases
- **Specificity**: concrete payoff without medical claims
- **Curiosity**: makes user want to click to see the “list/checklist/breakdown”
- **Low friction**: feels easy to do now
- **Policy safety**: passes hard gates

Only ship ads scoring **≥ 8/10**.

## How the loop updates this framework

Use these metrics to decide what to change:

- **Low thumbstop / low CTR** → hook and on-screen text (pattern interrupt + specificity)
- **Good CTR / low article engagement** → headline mismatch; rewrite headline/first paragraph
- **Good engagement / low widget clicks** → widget phrase options; tighten the 3–4 buttons and article “bridge” copy
- **Good widget clicks / weak vRPS** → phrase quality; adjust phrases toward advertiser-dense intent and remove low-value clusters

### “Stats” we actually trust (internal, not generic)

Instead of relying on generic internet benchmarks, we track *our* funnel:

- **Thumbstop proxy**: 1–3s view rate (creative)
- **CTR**: ad → article click-through (creative + headline alignment)
- **Article engagement**: scroll depth / time on page / next interaction (headline + first paragraph)
- **Widget CTR**: article → widget phrase click (article structure + phrase options)
- **Downstream value**: vRPS / Session ROAS (phrase quality + advertiser density)

Those are the only “statistics” that matter for optimizing this system.
